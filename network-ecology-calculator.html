The dual-layer principle
High amplification of verified content = positive signal. The network is functioning correctly. No intervention needed even if structural risk is high.

High amplification of unverified content = cascade risk. The same structurally powerful node is the problem. Immediate friction, down-ranking, context injection.

Structural risk tier and content status are computed independently. Their intersection — not any single metric — determines intervention priority. This preserves analytical neutrality: risk tiers make no claims about actor intent, content accuracy, or political orientation.
Implementation note — edge weights
This calculator uses rawWeight = interaction_type_weight × count — capturing both the type of interaction (mention=1 through hyperlink=4) and its observed frequency. This is the richer implementation for real-world media networks where a single actor may retweet another 50 times. The illustrative Table 2 values in the paper (e.g. degree(w)=4.75 for Influencer B) use a simplified edge-weight representation for expository clarity. The risk formula, centrality measures, content verification categories, and decision matrix are implemented exactly as specified in the paper.

Centrality measures (Section 4)
Weighted degree centrality — superspreader potential
C_D(v) = Σ w_vu / (N − 1)
Raw amplification capacity. Most sensitive to friction-based interventions. Separates broadcast hubs from local amplifiers.
Betweenness centrality — transmission vector
C_B(v) = Σ σ_st(v) / σ_st
Identifies actors on shortest paths between communities — determines which narrative version reaches which population.
Directed closeness centrality — reach potential
C_C(v) = (N − 1) / Σ d(v,t)
How efficiently a node can reach the full network. Identifies epidemic threshold position.
Weighted eigenvector centrality — cascade amplifier
A^(w) · C_E = λ · C_E
Captures cascade potential through connection to other high-influence nodes. Primary mechanism behind credibility laundering.
